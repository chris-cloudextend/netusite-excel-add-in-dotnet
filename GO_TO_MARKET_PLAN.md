## Xavi Financial Reporting Engine (FRE)

### Go-To-Market Plan

---

## 1. Strategic GTM Vision

**Objective**\
Position Xavi FRE as the Excel-native financial reporting engine for finance teams that need more robust, precise, and controllable reporting than native NetSuite tools can provide. Xavi FRE enables finance users to stay in Excel, leverage formulas they already trust, and produce highly tailored financial outputs without sacrificing governance or performance.

**Strategic Framing**\
It is about teams hitting the ceiling of native NetSuite reporting and seeking deeper control, higher fidelity layouts, and faster iteration cycles in Excel.

**Core Messages**

- Excel is the financial operating system for analysts and controllers.
- Xavi FRE unlocks full Excel modeling and layout control on top of live ERP data.
- Build financial reports the way finance teams actually think and work.
- Eliminate fragile exports and manual reconciliation.

---

## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Primary Competitor (NetSuite-Focused)

**Solution 7 (Zone & Co)**

- Excel plug-in providing live connections to NetSuite for financial reporting and planning.
- Strong adoption among NetSuite finance teams seeking Excel-based reporting.

**Xavi FRE Differentiation**

- Deeper Excel formula-driven logic rather than predefined report structures.
- Greater control over layout, calculations, and report composition.
- Positioned as a reporting engine rather than a reporting template tool.
- Does not require expensive ODBC driver

### Future and Adjacent Competitors (Beyond NetSuite)

These tools indicate where the broader market is heading: Excel as the analytical layer on top of financial systems.

- **Datarails**: Excel-centric FP&A platform integrating ERP data for planning and analysis.
- **F9 Financial Reporting (Infor)**: Excel-based GL reporting with formula-driven access across multiple ERPs.
- **Coefficient**: Live Excel connectors to systems including NetSuite, focused on simplicity and sync.
- **Cube**: FP&A platform bridging Excel with centralized data and controls.
- **Workiva / Vena**: Enterprise reporting platforms that retain Excel as a core modeling surface.

These vendors validate the demand for Excel-native financial workflows. Xavi FRE competes by being lighter weight, more reporting-focused, and more flexible at the formula and layout level.

---

## 3. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP

Finance teams that:

- Run NetSuite as their core ERP.
- Rely heavily on Excel for financial reporting, analysis, and modeling.
- Need precise control over financial layouts, calculations, and presentation.
- Operate in multi-entity, multi-currency, or complex reporting environments.

**Company Profile**

- Mid-market to enterprise.
- Finance teams with dedicated FP&A or financial analyst roles.

### Key User Personas

- CFOs seeking faster close cycles and clearer financial narratives.
- Controllers who need repeatable, auditable financial reports.
- FP&A leaders building custom management reporting.
- Financial analysts responsible for monthly, quarterly, and ad hoc reporting.

---

## 4. Why Build in Excel vs Native NetSuite Reporting

### Native NetSuite Limitations

- Rigid report layouts and formatting constraints.
- Limited calculation flexibility compared to Excel formulas.
- High friction for iteration and scenario analysis.
- Heavy reliance on saved searches and technical constructs.

### Xavi FRE Advantages

- Full Excel formula engine for calculations, allocations, and transformations.
- Pixel-level control over layouts and report structures.
- Rapid iteration without IT involvement.
- Live refresh against governed ERP data.

Excel becomes the presentation, calculation, and modeling layer. NetSuite remains the system of record.

## 5. Go-To-Market Phases

### Phase 1: Precision Reporting for NetSuite Finance Teams

**Target Audience**\
NetSuite finance teams who already live in Excel and are actively looking for:

- More robust financial reporting than native NetSuite can provide.
- Finer control over report layouts and financial logic.
- A scalable replacement for manual exports and brittle spreadsheets.

**Key Insight**\
These teams are not transitioning away from Excel. They are doubling down on Excel and demanding that ERP data meet them where they work.

**Marketing Focus**

- Position Xavi FRE as the answer to NetSuite reporting ceilings.
- Content showing complex financial statements built entirely in Excel.
- Comparisons highlighting layout control and formula depth versus native NetSuite reports.
- Webinars focused on real-world finance workflows, not dashboards.

### Phase 2: Demand Generation and Adoption

- Target FP&A and controller-led buying motions.
- Emphasize time savings, accuracy, and analyst autonomy.
- Offer guided trials with real customer reports recreated in Excel.
- Partner with NetSuite consultants.

### Phase 3: Expansion and Platform Pull-Through

- Expand usage across departments and reporting use cases.
- Introduce standardized report libraries and best-practice templates.
- Position Xavi FRE as the foundation for broader Excel-based analytics beyond financial statements.
